Jalapenos

Quick overview

Jalapenos are a great crop that can add a little spice to your summertime dishes! Only needing a pot or two, jalapenos love the heat and actually prefer being a bit dry, so they are relatively low maintenance and are just fun, versatile plants!

Varieties

Senorita, Sierra Fuego, Mucho Nacho

How to Plant

Plant seeds just 1/4 inch deep (you can just cover lightly with seed-starting mix). Water in and keep peppers moist (though never wet). Ensure your seedlings have good airflow and aren’t crowded. Once big enough, replant them into a bigger pot or plant them outside.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight.
- Water 3 times a week with ⅛ cup per seedling and ½ cup for adult plants, just to keep the soil slightly moist. Adjust according to what you see, but remember chili peppers HATE sitting in the wet.
- Let the top inch or two of soil dry out before watering again.
- Trellises required for vining, decent amount of pests, constant harvesting required.
- Best to buy transplants for Needham. Or grow, starting growing 8-10 weeks before last spring freeze.

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 9-23rd, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. Ensure that soil and nighttime temperatures reach at least 60°F (15°C), and then plant outside spacing them 14 to 24 inches apart.
