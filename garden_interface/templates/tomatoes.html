Tomatoes

Quick overview

Tomatoes are a staple in any summer garden. With tons of varieties, tomatoes are a versatile crop that come in tons of different sizes, flavors, and even colors! While you should spend time researching which variety works best for what you want, no matter which variety you get, make sure they stay well watered and have at least a couple of square feet in the sun so that they produce as much as possible!

Varieties

Bush. vining (this is a ask farmers)

How to Plant

If you are starting tomatoes from seed, sow indoors 6 weeks before the last recommended planting date below. Sow seeds 1/2-inch deep in small trays.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight.
- Water 3 times a week, with ½ cup per seedling or 1-3 cups per adult plant.
- Choose large pots if planting inside to allow plenty of space for the roots. Tomatoes don’t like being crowded.
- Use loose, well-draining soil.

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 9th-30th, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. Transplant your seedlings (or your nursery-grown plants) into the ground outdoors after all danger of frost has passed and the soil is at least 60°F. Place each root ball deep enough so that the bottom leaves are just above the surface of the soil. Roots will grow all along the plant’s stem underground. Plant seedlings 2 to 3 feet apart.
