Become the best gardener that you can be

Whether you’re tired of running to the store for tomatoes or want to grow flowers to beautify your home, there are many benefits to learning how to garden. Proper tactics for nurturing plant life can save you time and money, and also become a highly-enjoyable hobby. If you’re starting with nothing, you may ask, “How can I learn gardening?” It does not require any special kind of person — just practice, supplies, and knowledge.

Cucumbers

Cucumbers are a fun crop to incorporate in your garden or simply in large pots. There are two main types of cucumbers, vining and bush cucumbers, both of which consistently grow cucumbers throughout the entire growing season. If you're growing a vining variety, make sure to also buy a trellis (or grow it near a slotted fence!) for it to grow on, but bush varieties should probably be planted in actual garden beds. Make sure to regularly harvest the cucumbers and water them frequently, but there are tons of different ways you'll be able to use them!

Jalapenos

Jalapenos are a great crop that can add a little spice to your summertime dishes! Only needing a pot or two, jalapenos love the heat and actually prefer being a bit dry, so they are relatively low maintenance and are just fun, versatile plants!

Lettuce

Water: medium. maintenance: low. sun: 6-8 direct. space, 20+ square feet Lettuce is a great summertime crop that requires a bit of space but is easy to grow and can be a great part of tons of fresh recipes! Pests can be a problem, so make sure you choose a variety that is pest resistant and make sure that you have raised bed gardens or a fence to keep out rabbits and other rodents!

Tomatoes

Tomatoes are a staple in any summer garden. With tons of varieties, tomatoes are a versatile crop that come in tons of different sizes, flavors, and even colors! While you should spend time researching which variety works best for what you want, no matter which variety you get, make sure they stay well watered and have at least a couple of square feet in the sun so that they produce as much as possible!
