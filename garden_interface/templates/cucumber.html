Quick overview

Cucumbers are a fun crop to incorporate in your garden or simply in large pots. There are two main types of cucumbers, vining and bush cucumbers, both of which consistently grow cucumbers throughout the entire growing season. If you're growing a vining variety, make sure to also buy a trellis (or grow it near a slotted fence!) for it to grow on, but bush varieties should probably be planted in actual garden beds. Make sure to regularly harvest the cucumbers and water them frequently, but there are tons of different ways you'll be able to use them!

Varieties

Vining, bush

How to Plant

Start pepper seeds 1/4 of an inch deep, three to a pot filled with potting mix. For faster germination, maintain soil at 70°F (21°C) or above.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight
- Water 3 times a week with around ½ cup of water per seedling or a 1-1 ½ cups of water per plant. Allow soil to dry out between waterings
- Trellises required for vining, decent amount of pests, constant harvesting required
- The blossoms are extremely sensitive so if it’s too hot outside use shades to maintain adequate temperature

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 16-30th, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. Make the transplant holes 3 to 4 inches deep and 12 to 18 inches apart in the row. Space the rows 2 to 3 feet apart.
