Rosemary

Quick overview

Rosemary is a delicious herb that is a staple in tons of dishes, marinades, and seasonings. Like most herbs, it doesn't really require any maintenance and only needs a bit of water. Said to help strengthen memory, plant it in a pot or two and enjoy plenty of tasty rosemary!

How to Plant

It’s strongly recommended to start new rosemary plants from cuttings taken from established plants, as seeds and seedlings grow extremely slow. Plant cuttings 1-2 inches deep,in well-drained soil. Keep it moist while seeds germinate and roots develop.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight.
- Water 2 times a week with ⅛ cup per seedling and ½ cup for adult plant, just to keep the soil slightly moist.
- Harvest young stems and leaves for the freshest taste.
- During winter bring potted rosemary inside.

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 9-30th, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. Plant 1-2 inches deep, 1 per square foot, in the full sun to part shade.
