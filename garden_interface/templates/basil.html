Basil

Quick overview

Basil is a low-maintenance, tasty herb that can flourish in even a small pot! Make sure to cut the flowers to give more energy to the leaves , and also make sure to not overwater it! Basil doesn't like constantly having wet roots.

Varieties

Sweet, Purple, Lemon, Thai

How to Plant

Plant seeds no more than 1/4-inch deep, preferably in seedling pots. Once seedlings emerge and have 2-3 pairs of true leaves, replant seedlings to one plant every 10 to 12 inches or separate them into small pots if growing inside.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight
- Water 3 times a week, just to keep the soil moist
- Just pruning, few pests, no constant harvesting necessary
When plant is 4-6in start removing the center set of leaves at the growing tip to encourage branching resulting in bushier plant

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 2-23rd, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. To plant outside, wait until the soil has warmed up (at least 50°F (10°C). If weather is going to be cold make sure to harvest your basil beforehand or it will die
