Oregano

Quick overview

Oregano is a delicious herb that can be used in a variety of dishes! It's a very low maintenance plant that doesn't need much water or care, and you only need one or two pots to have more than enough for any meal. Keep it in a well-lit area and occasionally pinch off the flower buds but other than that, just plant and enjoy having a tasty herb!

Varieties

Greek

How to Plant

Seeds should be planted ¼ inch deep. Sprinkle soil over the seeds and tap the soil to firm. Water oregano seeds in well. Maintain consistently moist soil throughout the germination period of 8-14 days and until plants get established.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight. - Water 2 times a week with ⅛ cup per seedling and ½ cup for adult plant.
- Plant well past the last chance of frost. Regular trimming is good but not necessary. Head the buds.
-Harvest the leaves once the plant is several inches tall. This will encourage new growth. Don’t harvest more than one-third of the plant at a time.

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 2-23rd, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. Plant 8 to 10 inches apart. The plants will grow 1 to 2 feet tall and spread about 18 inches.
