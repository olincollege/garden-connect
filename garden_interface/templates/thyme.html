Thyme

Quick overview

Thyme is a tasty herb that is easy to grow and produces tons of leaves that can be used in plenty of seasonings! It doesn't need too much more, and basically needs no maintenance after planting, but plant a pot or two to enjoy a consistent supply of delicious thyme!

Varieties

French/English, Lemon, Caraway

How to Plant

It’s hard to grow thyme from seeds because of slow, uneven germination. It’s easier to buy the plants from a garden center or take some cuttings from a friend. Plant cuttings 1-2 inches deep,in well-drained soil. Keep it moist while seeds germinate and roots develop.

Caring tips

- Place in direct sunlight.
- Water 2 times a week with ¼ cup per seedling and 1 - 1 ½ cup for adult plant.
- Don't overwater, water deeply only when the soil is completely dry.
- Dead head buds.
- Harvest thyme just before the plant flowers by cutting off the top five to six inches of growth. Leave the tough, woody parts.

Planting Outside

Ideal time to plant outdoes is May 2-23rd, but it can be grown any time in the pot inside. Plant cuttings or young thyme plants any time after the ground temperature reaches 70°F. Space young plants 12 to 24 inches apart, depending upon the specific variety. The plants should grow 6 to 12 inches in height.
